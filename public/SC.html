Sword Combat

Sword Combat Rules

(The rules to sword combat are very similar to point sparring rules)

  1. All bouts are 1 minute for all ages
  2. First to 5 points or who ever is ahead when time permits, will be declare the winner.
  3. Bouts can only commence when the Centre Referee calls start.
  4. Bouts can only finish when the Centre Referee calls stop.
  5. The Centre referee is the only one who can start or stop bouts and issue warnings

Scoring points

  • Hit to the head scores two points
  • All other parts of the body score one point, except you cannot score to the fingers and up to the wrist area. (Note: From above the wrist area is classified as body part)

Legal Scoring Techniques

You must keep the sword lower than your eye level when swinging or attacking your opponent. Stabbing type swings can only be done to the stomach area only.

Illegal Techniques

You cannot swing the sword back past eye level, as this would be classed as a baseball swing. In the event of two warnings this will lead to point over. No talking back to the Referee or disputing his/her final call. Control of the weapon at all times must be applied (no exception).

Equipment Required

Headgear, Mouth guard and Full Martial Arts Uniform must be worn (No Exceptions).

Note

Competitors must supply their own equipment for this event. Some promoters will supply equipment
