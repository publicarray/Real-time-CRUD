Sumo

Sumo Rules

  1. Bouts for competitor six years and under will be 1 minute long Note: A bout is equal to 1 point.)
  2. Bouts for competitors above six years will be 1 and half minutes long (Note: A bout is equal to 1 point.)
  3. Competitor/s who is ahead after best of three (3) scoring bouts or who ever is ahead when time permits, will be declared the winner
  4. Where weight categories are required, ensure your correct weight is recorded within the closet kilo rounded up on your entry form.
    E.g. If you are 45.6kg recorded your weight on your form as 46kg not 45.6kg, as 46kg will be to the closet kilo.
  5. You may also on the day of the tournament be requiring to be weighed by the officials, before you can compete.
  6. Karate Kung fu or Taekwondo Gi with long sleeves must me worn for this event no short sleeves, T shirts or similar are not allowed.

Starting Position

Both competitors must start by doing the Sumo stomp and walking up to each other. They must face each other, and place both arms on their opponents opposite shoulders. Bouts can only commence when the Centre Referee calls start. Bouts can only finish when the Centre Referee calls stop. The Centre referee is the only one who can start or stop bouts and issue warnings.

Legal Scoring Techniques

You can push your opponent out of the ring; both feet of your opponent must be out to score a point. You can pull your opponent down by the shoulders or arms; a point will be scored, where any part of their body touch the mat, except their feet.

No throws of any type allowed. No Pinching of opponentâ€™s body allowed (If pitching occurs, the opponent causing the pitching will be disqualified). No talking back to the Referee or disputing his/her final call. No charging at your opponent at any time. No Lifting of the leg of any type to an opponent. No Sweeping of the legs allowed.

Equipment Required

Headgear, Mouth guard and Full Martial Arts Uniform must be worn Karate Kung fu or Taekwondo Gi with long sleeves (No Exceptions).
